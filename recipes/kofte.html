Köfte

Homemade Turkish Meatballs known as kofte are the best things to happen on your dinner table. These are spicy, tender and perfectly juicy. It’s not a secret any more to make meatballs at home that are much tastier than store-bought ones.

  • onion grated
  • 2 cloves garlic crushed/finely grated
  • 2 tablespoon mint finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoon parsley finely chopped
  • 1 lb ground lamb 450g lamb mince
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 egg
  1. I like to have half & half mixture of ground beef and ground lamb in my meatballs; you need at least about 25% of the meat content as ground lamb for that delicious, melt-in-the-mouth meatball taste.
  2. Our koftes have quite a generous grated onion in it, as well as parsley, stale bread, 1 egg and salt & ground black pepper seasoning. My mother includes a generous amount of stale bread crumbs in the mixture and that makes koftes taste wonderful and moist.
  3. Mix the ingredients (except the meat) first; that softens the onions and the mixture blends with the meat better.
  4. Cook one meatball on the pan or grill first to check the seasoning. If they need more salt or pepper, add some to the rest of the meatballs. Good seasoning is essential.
